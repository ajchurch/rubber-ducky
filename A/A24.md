# A24. Teach your family about cybersecurity topic of your choice.

Since my family live over 400kms away, I've decided to meet with my mother on a video call to discuss the cybersecurity topic of Password management and account security.

Keeping accounts secure are super important in the prevention of personal and financial information from being hacked and teaching my family about some simple and easy adopt things to make sure they're secure online.

I created a very quick document which summarises some of the key points that I wanted my family to learn about.
* Strong passwords (how to make a good password and never to reuse them)
* 2FA (ensuring primarily the most important accounts like emails, social media, and banking have at the bare minimum 2 factor authentication or even better if it's multifactor)
* Use a Password Manager (my family use an email service which has a password manager inbuilt, so just encouraging them to use it when making passwords, and new accounts) 

Here is the document I gave them: https://docs.google.com/document/d/1RgHhosInETnwmXUbkkVTL9qz_fhEpOl4fT19KP-baRY/edit?usp=sharing

I also briefly discussed [HaveIBeenPwned](https://haveibeenpwned.com/), a site which lets you put your email in to check if it has been in a data breach. I then encouraged my mother to check if any of her (and my other not so tech savvy family members') email accounts were breached and to take actionable steps such as changing her passwords on those sites.
