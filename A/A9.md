# A9. Discover privacy technique used online.

A privacy technique used online example is [iCloud Private Relay](https://www.apple.com/au/legal/privacy/data/en/icloud-relay/). This is Apple's internet privacy service offered to iCloud+ users that uses something called Dual-Hop Architecture to ensure that no single party, including Apple, can see both who you are (your IP address) and where you are going (the website you’re visiting).

## What is Dual-Hop Architecture?

When you browse with Safari, your request doesn't go straight to the website. Instead, it takes two "hops" through separate relays:
Your Device: Encrypts the web address (the URL) so your Internet Service Provider (ISP) can’t see it.
Relay 1 (The Ingress Proxy): Operated by Apple.
What it sees: Your real IP address (who you are).
What it doesn't see: The encrypted website address.

Action: It strips your IP and replaces it with a generic one from your approximate region.

Relay 2 (The Egress Proxy): Operated by Third-Party Partners (like Cloudflare).
What it sees: The website address you want to visit.
What it doesn't see: Your real IP address (it only sees the generic one from Apple).

Action: It decrypts the destination and forwards your request to the website.

iCloud Private Relay is similar to a VPN but its scope is typically limited to only Safari or some other unencrypted web traffic on the apple device. It doesn't let you 'spoof' your location like most VPNs either so its essentially a more simplistic VPN that is native and run in the background without the user having to worry about anything that can act as a layer of privacy protection to someone even if they know nothing about cyber safety.
