# A4. Discover a vulnerable website.

[Spotify](https://spotify.com)'s Password Reset has a low-severity vulnerability of telling the user if the email is linked to a spotify account or not. While at first this might not seem like something that matters, this vulnerability allows an attacker to map out a service's user base through Information Disclosure. By confirming which email addresses are registered, Spotify essentially hands over a verified list of targets.

Some potential risks would be:

Credential Stuffing - Since many people use the same password for Spotify as they do for their email or social media, an attacker can use a leaked database to find potential matches. If they can confirm the email exists on Spotify first, they can focus their energy on brute-forcing only the active accounts.

Privacy Exposure - Many users prefer to keep their listening habits or account presence private. Knowing someone has a Spotify account could allow an attacker to search for their public playlists, potentially revealing their real name, musical tastes, or even their physical location if their Spotify account is linked to another third-party service like BeReal.

Phishing - Attackers can send fake "Payment Failed" or "Security Alert" emails. Because the user knows they definitely have a Spotify account, they are significantly more likely to click the link and enter their credit card details or login credentials into a fake site.
