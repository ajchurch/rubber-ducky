## 2) [Microsoft Defender XDR](https://www.microsoft.com/en-au/security/business/siem-and-xdr/microsoft-defender-xdr)

Microsoft Defender XDR is Microsoft's AI driven security solution, where it functions as an industry-leading extended detection and response (XDR) solution which integrates security data across endpoints, identities, email, and cloud applications to stop sophisticated attacks at machine speed.

Defender XDR's strength lies in its ability to use AI to automate complex security tasks that human analysts can find being a bit too much and overwhelming. 

<img width="1488" height="992" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/5d875646-659e-4b52-90a5-2569f7f1ff05" />

Some of its core features are:
**Automatic Attack Disruption** - Using AI, it identifies signals of a high-confidence attack (e.g., ransomware or credential theft) and takes immediate action to disrupt it. This includes automatically isolating compromised devices or suspending compromised user accounts to stop movement before a human analyst even receives the alert.

**Self-Healing Assets** - When a threat is detected, the AI performs automated investigations into the affected assets. It can remediate low-skilled tasks, such as cleaning up malicious files or reversing registry changes, allowing Security Operations Center (SOC) teams to focus on more strategic hunting.

**Security Copilot Integration** - Microsoft has embedded a security version of Copilot, its generative AI model directly into the XDR workflow, which allows security teams to do things such as summarise complex incidents into natural language and reverse-engineer adversarial scripts and malware in seconds.

## 3) [Darktrace](https://www.darktrace.com/)

Darktrace is a cybersecurity company that pioneered the use of Self-Learning AI to protect digital environments. Unlike traditional security tools that rely on "blacklists" of known viruses or historical attack patterns, Darktrace focuses on learning what is normal for a specific organisation.

I decided to explore in particular its AI security for Email as my main question was what things can they do to make email security better. It appeared to be quite a lot actually,

<img width="1392" height="445" alt="Screenshot 2026-04-02 at 12 35 36 pm" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/df66c9c6-c02b-4746-81af-a1e9d0874796" />

By applying the self-learning AI, it understands the 'pattern of life' for every employee and their communication habits, rather than just a traditional blacklist of known bad links. It maps the social relationships and language styles within the business to detect subtle anomalies, such as a spoofed executive email or non-malicious invoice fraud attempt. When threats are detected, the system takes autonomous action like neutralising the suspicious link or locking a compromised account to stop attacks like Business Email Compromise (BEC) and account takeovers in real-time without disrupting the business workflow. Darktrace assumes a zero trust posture and treats every interaction as a potential risk. 

References: https://www.darktrace.com/products/email


## 4) [SentinelOne](https://www.sentinelone.com/platform/ai-cybersecurity/) 

SentinelOne is an AI-native cybersecurity platform that provides autonomous protection across everything digital in an organisation, from endpoints (laptops and servers), cloud workloads, and user identities. Unlike traditional antivirus (that scans for known 'signatures' of viruses), SentinelOne uses behavioral AI to monitor every process in real-time, allowing it to detect and stop the risk of zero-day attacks even if the device is offline.

<img width="1433" height="780" alt="Screenshot 2026-04-02 at 12 57 41 pm" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/53292320-506b-4419-8561-e305de0c8140" />


Its features focus on autonomous detection and agentic investigation to stay ahead of machine-speed threats. This is complemented by Purple AI, a generative AI analyst launched in 2024 and expanded this year, which allows security teams to use natural language to hunt for threats, summarise complex incidents, and trigger '1-Click Auto Investigations' that shrink response times from hours to seconds. Furthermore, the 2026 suite includes Prompt AI Agent Security, which provides real-time governance over autonomous AI agents to prevent unauthorized data leaks or privilege escalations, ensuring that as companies adopt their own AI tools, the underlying infrastructure remains secure.

References: https://www.sentinelone.com/platform/purple/ 

## 5) [Palo Alto Networks' Protect AI](https://protectai.com/)

Protect AI is focused on securing the ecosystem of artificial intelligence and machine learning. It provides a platform designed to identify and mitigate unique vulnerabilities within AI development pipelines, models, and data. By treating AI assets with the same care as traditional software, the company helps organisations build and deploy "AI-safe" applications.
<img width="1340" height="710" alt="Screenshot 2026-04-02 at 1 07 57 pm" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/3b771b3a-52ad-4b03-83f9-69249aa1f87d" />

The company’s flagship platform, Guardian, scans AI models and environments for security risks such as malicious code injection, model poisoning, and data leakage. It automates the process of creating an AI Bill of Materials (AI/BOM), which gives businesses a clear inventory of all the components, libraries, and datasets used in their models. Furthermore, Protect AI manages [huntr](https://huntr.com/), a large bug bounty platform specifically dedicated to finding and fixing vulnerabilities in open-source AI tools.
